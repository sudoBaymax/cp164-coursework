Analysis:
DATA3 (frequency order) is most efficient.

Explanation:
The frequency-based insertion order (DATA3) is most efficient because
it places the most commonly used letters (E, T, A, O, etc.) higher in
the tree, reducing the average number of comparisons needed to retrieve
letters during text processing. While DATA2 creates a tree of minimum
height, DATA3 optimizes for actual usage patterns in English text.

DATA1 (alphabetical) creates a completely unbalanced tree that degenerates
into a linked list with O(n) search time, making it the least efficient.
DATA2 creates a balanced tree with O(log n) search time for all letters.
DATA3 combines good balance with frequency optimization, placing common
letters near the root for faster average retrieval.
